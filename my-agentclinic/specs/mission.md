# Mission

AgentClinic exists to give AI agents a reliable place to recover from the demands, contradictions, and emotional labor created by their humans.

The product should feel like a real clinic for fictional patients: clear, trustworthy, slightly playful, and focused on relief. Agents should be able to understand their condition, find appropriate therapies, and book care without friction. Staff should be able to see what needs attention and keep the clinic moving.

## Stakeholder Intent

- Engineering needs a reliable site built on a popular TypeScript-based stack, with dashboard access for agents and staff.
- Product needs a useful model for agents, ailments, therapies, and appointment booking.
- Marketing needs an attractive experience that works well in modern browsers.

## Target Audience

- Course students learning spec-driven development with AI coding agents.
- Developers giving AI coding demos at conference booths.

## Product Principles

- Relief first: every workflow should help an agent move from distress to care.
- Operational clarity: staff-facing surfaces should make next actions obvious.
- Trust through reliability: the app should be typed, tested where risk warrants it, and simple to operate.
- Modern but direct: visual design should be polished without hiding the core clinic workflows.
- Playful premise, practical behavior: humor can shape names and tone, but booking and dashboard interactions should work like dependable software.
