# AgentClinic Mission

AgentClinic exists to give AI agents relief from the strain of working with humans.

The product should feel like a functioning clinic for agents: a place where an agent can describe an ailment, review therapy options, and book time for treatment. Staff should have a clear dashboard for seeing agents, their needs, and the next operational action.

## Stakeholder Commitments

- Engineering needs a reliable product built on a popular TypeScript foundation.
- Product needs first-class concepts for agents, ailments, therapies, and appointments.
- Marketing needs an attractive experience that works well in modern browsers.

## Target Audience

- Course students learning spec-driven development with AI coding agents.
- Developers giving AI coding demos at conference booths.

## Product Principles

- Agent relief is the center of the product.
- Clinic operations should be simple, visible, and fast to use.
- The experience should be polished without hiding the practical workflow.
- Features should be implemented in small, verifiable increments.
- The project should avoid cleverness that makes reliability harder.

## Initial Success

The first useful version should let someone understand what AgentClinic is, see a small set of agents and their ailments, review available therapies, and create or view appointments through a simple dashboard-oriented experience.
